📐How we got these numbers (plain English)

  1. We pulled every Goose setlist back to 2018 — 550 shows, 323 unique songs — from El Goose (the band's setlist archive). The 18 shows from 2026 carry more weight than older data because the rotation has changed since Everything Must Go and Chain Yer Dragon.
  2. Each song's starting point is "how often does it show up?" — counted across the 2026 tour and the last 50 shows combined. Heaviest rotators (Give It Time, Animal, Hungersite) hit ~30% of recent shows. That's the per-song baseline.
  3. MSG is the big stage. We bumped up odds for "set-2 launch pad" jams (Hungersite, Thatch, Madhuvan), encore staples (Arcadia, Give It Time), and added a real possibility of a cover and a bustout from the deep catalog. Stuart Bogie's horns showed up at MSG 2025 — bet on them returning.
  4. Goose almost never repeats songs across back-to-back nights. We measured this from 105 historical 2-night pairs since 2018 — only 0.89% of N1 songs come back on N2. So Night 2 picks fade hard for anything we expect on Night 1.
  5. BIG MODERN! drops June 12 — seven days before MSG N1. Songs already debuted from the album (Good2B, MEDIA, Torero, SALT) get a release-week boost, and there's a real shot at a live debut of one of the unplayed album tracks (Scavenger, POP, Good Times // End Times).
  6. The percentages sum to the show's length. A two sets + encore show is ~18 slots total — and the probabilities you see all together describe how that 18-song pie gets sliced. No song can clear 50% because Goose's catalog is too deep for any one song to be a true "lock".
One thing to remember: the deep catalog matters. Even the #1 pick sits well under 50% because ~60% of every Goose show's slots come from outside the top two dozen rotators. Treat these as relative odds — ranking matters more than the exact %. For the full math, see the Methodology page.
